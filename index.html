    What is Homebrew?

    Homebrew usually refers to software that is not authorized by Microsoft. It allows you to run homebrew games, tools like save editing and backup, and emulators for various older systems.

    What does this guide install?

    There are various options of exploits to use, from the free, but legacy King Kong shader exploit, to the most superior, but costs money and requires soldering, RGH modchip.

    There are a lot of variables required in finding what exploit suits you, as all have their pros, and cons.

    What can I do with a Modded Console?

    • Get a customized dashboard with more features.
    • Get custom themes
    • Backup, edit, and restore saves for many games.
    • Play games for older systems with various emulators, using RetroArch or other standalone emulators.
    • Dump your game discs to a hard-drive/USB-drive, and play them.
    • Play your Xbox games on your 360 with a mod of the built in Xbox emulator adding higher compatability

    What do I need to know before starting?

    • Before beginning the guide, you must know the risks of 360 hacking: EVERY time you modify your system, there is always the potential for an UNRECOVERABLE brick. They’re rare, but still a possibility so make sure you follow ALL directions EXACTLY.
    • This guide will work on all Xbox 360 Phats, Xbox 360 Slim, and Xbox 360 E devices in all regions and firmwares
    • If everything goes according to plan, you will lose no data and end up with everything that you started with (games, Live Account, saves, etc will be preserved).
